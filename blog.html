Prayer Life

May 1st, 2016

Monday through Friday, a friend of mine sends out a group message to a couple dozen of his friends. Each one has been inspiring and uplifting, but this Friday's was such an essential topic in the Christian walk.

"Prayer is a powerful thing. It can be so easy to take for granted that we have direct access to the creator of the universe anytime we WANT. So it's unfortunate that most of our prayers come at a time that we NEED."

"Now don't get me wrong, prayer is definitely designed for our needs, but it's also designed to strengthen our relationship with God, too, communicating our needs and wants is only part of it."

"'The Lord is near to all who call on Him in truth." -Psalms 145:18

"Just as you desire a relationship with God, He also desires a relationship with you. Just like an earthly relationship, you have to spend time with it, it doesn't happen overnight, but the more time you spend with someone, the better you get to know them."

"So how do we do that? What so we pray for?"

"Well, we covered needs and wants, how about prayers of forgiveness? Prayers of thanks? Prayers for others? Don't just hope for wisdom, or intelligence, or self control, or joy, or a loving heart, or patience, etc, pray for it!"

"'This is the confidence we have in approaching God: that if we ask anything according to His will, he hears.'-1 John 5:14"

"Let God be the first person you run to in times of need, thanks, apology, you name it. You get out of it what you put into it!"

"TAKEAWAY: God desperately wants to know you and for you to know Him. Ask and you shall recieve, but like any relationship, you must cultivate. Start creating a healthy and active prayer life."

"PRAYER: God, instill in me a vibrant and open prayer life, help me to come to you not just in times of need but through everything for I know that you are the only the only true and just thing in my life, develop in me a yearning for your presence and hand a desire to hand in my life!"
